Desert Environment

Overview

This Desert Environment was made completely from scratch. All Meshes, Materials, and even the Design were made by me. I used Blender, Substance Designer and UE4.


Substance Designer

My favourite Materials are the Sand and the Wood. The Sand I simply like, because of the overall look and because it tiles so well. The Wood however, is great because it is so versatile. By layering Detail and by tweaking setting, I was able to create multiple variants very quickly.

Terrain

The Terrain Material utilizes Tri-planar Mapping without any need for handpainting. On flat surfaces, Sand is displayed. On sufaces which are a bit sloped, the sand is still displayed with decreased normals and AmbientOcclusion. On steep slopes, Rock is shown.
Admittedly, there is a problem. Like all tri-planar mapped meshes, my Terrain shows stretching. To hide that stretching, I placed Meshes on the affected Areas.

Progress

My first idea was to create a Scene inspired by Dark Souls in one week. The bulk of my Reference were Screenshots from the actual games and pictures of ruins and Gothic buildings.
After I finished my first blockout - when I was sculpting the Terrain - I got the idea to place the scene in the middle of a sand storm. Even before I textured my Meshes, I made Materials for the Terrain. After that, I made sure to submerge parts of the scene in sand. When I was happy with the overall feel of the scene, I added more Materials, Details and Effects.
