Overview

Two games made on two different GameJams but using the same websocket technology. One PC has to start the Game and everyone can join in just by visiting the PC's IP.
No App required!

Using Socket.IO, Docker, a server written by my team member Daniel years ago, and an old javascript-controller made this possible.
The controller's data (id, Vector2, and dis/connection events) is stringified with JSON and send over the server to the Unity application. This data is then destringified and processed.

Multiplayer Snake - Team Size: 2

It's just like regular Snake but missing a grid, with massive multiplayer and teams. One team doesn't fight against the other - they even help each other! Your true enemies are the members of your own team.
Ice had to avoid ice or it shatters.
Fire has to avoid fire or it gets out of control.

As a ice player you can help the fire players by extinguishing their burning trails and give them space to move. Fire can melt ice trails and healps the other team. At the end only one player per team remains. They win the game!

The websocket implementation was done by Daniel and all of the gameplay by me.

Blobby Party - Team Size: 5

Blobby volley is a 2D game in which two players play volley ball against each other. But why stop there? We wanted to create a similar game and played it with about 20 people.

This time we tried to resolve some problems we had with Multiplayer Snake. Players didn't know which was their character most of the time, even though each had it's own tint. To solve that we gave the javascript-controller a random color as a background. This color was then send along other data to the game which gave the character the exact same color. We also added two buttons to test how much is doable with our approach.

My main task was to use the input from the websocket and make the player characters controllable with it.
