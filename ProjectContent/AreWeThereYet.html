Are we there yet?
July 2018

Overview

'Are we there yet?' is a game for PC and mobile made in July 2018 and polished in January 2019.

You have to race across the highway to quickly reach a restroom for your children. Reach the next service station, causing as few traffic accidents as possible. If you fail, your carpet will be ruined!


Car spawning

Spawning a few dozen cars alone doesn't create performance problems. Nevertheless, I always strive to make my code performant.
In the following video you can see that cars and road-pieces are only active as long as they are needed:

In order to retain my ability to adjust the camera later, I didn't hardcode the spawn- and despawn-positions, but made them dependant on the view. A car will always spawn right above the top end of the screen and despawn right below it. By casting two rays from the camera, I determine where the screen ends and add a buffer to it, so the cars don't visibly plop in or out of existance.



Development changes

Initially, I wanted normal car controls, enabling the player to move in any direction on a 2D plane.
This version is still present on the repository in the scene 'OldCar'.

The weird cube sticking out of the back right tire is for visualizing the rotation of the wheels.
When I decided the game would play on a small highway, I quickly decided that this kind of movement was just not fitting. It was a difficult decision to redo almost all the controls, but restricting the them made the game much more comfortable to play. Now the player can just turn a limited amount to either side and when nothing is pressed, the car will align itself with the road automatically.
