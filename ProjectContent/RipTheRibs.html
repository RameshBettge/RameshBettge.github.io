What I made

VR Controls

All the aspects of VR interaction are solely made by me. Even the input system was designed by me.
The Oculus Integration from the Unity Asset Store was NOT used.

These are the essential VR interactions in Rip the Ribs:

- Grabbing
- Trowing
- Shooting
- Turning
- Moving (Teleportation)

All interaction should feel intuitive and believable. Buckets are always gripped at the handle and pistols always point forwards, when picked up.
Other objects, like enemy skulls, will keep their orientation after gripped, so immersion is not destroyed when they suddenly face in another direction.
Except for the Teleportation, no action feels 'gamey'. Everything feels natural, to maximize immersion.


Sea and Bouyancy

Creating the sea was a challenge.
There were four steps to take:

- Creating the sea's mesh
- Creating 'waves'
- Making the boats float on top of the waves
- Rotating the boats to make everything believable.
- Moving the boats towards the players' ship and boarding it.

What makes me the proudest is the rotation.
To create the it, I sampled the sea's height at four points (front, back, left right) and calculated a rotation from it.
An average rotation is then calculated from the samples of the last frames.
The more frames are sample, the smoother the boat will be. If just a few samples are used, the boat will move more wildly and excitingly.


Destruction of Skeletons

If arms or legs of the skeletons are shot, they will detach. The bones will then be thrown into the direction of the shot.
Bones near to the impact will be thrown farther.

Every bone (except for hand bones, which are grouped together for performance) has a 'GrabbableObject' script, so they can be picked up.
To give me the ability to easily change different attributes, like Rigidbody drag, or custom torque-calculation when thrown, I created an editor script.
The editor script enables me to set parameters once and apply them to all bones.
