Procedural Corridor
September 2018

Overview

Why this was made

The Procedural Corridor is the result of my first experimentation with the Unreal Engine.
In school, we learned how to generate rooms for a dungeon. The one major thing missing was how to generate the corridors to connect these rooms.
How to generate the mesh for a corridor while only knowing where it ends and where it starts? To solve that, I created this project.



What I learned

By making just one single feature, and no gameplay, I learned a lot. Here are some examples:

- C++ syntax
- Usage of .h and .cpp files
- Usage of Pointers
- Instantiation of Actors and Actor-Components
- Custom Editor creation
- Calling functions inside the Editor


These one thing I'm most proud of, is one particular skill I improved:

Problem Solving

On multiple occassions I got really stuck. The problem was, that all of my friends and even my teacher had almost no experience with UE4 and C++.
This meant that I had to find out about various little quirks of the Engine and Language all on my own.
It was a bit frustrating some times but in the end it really helped me advance myself.
